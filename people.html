Brandon Rodrigue

I started my undergraduate career in the department of neuroscience at Carleton University in 2015. Following a few successful years, I ended up moving back home and completing my degree at Brock University with first-class honours in neurobiology. During my time at Brock, I worked under Dr. Aleksander Necakov where we were looking at developing a novel CRISPR-dCas9 construct that would allow for easy insertion and removal of multiple short guide (sg)RNAs. The purpose of which would be to then have an efficient system to screen many sgRNAs in a short period of time for sufficient repression of the target gene.

Following this, I decided to return to Carleton University to pursue a graduate degree in neuroscience. I joined the AAV lab in the fall semester of 2020 as a Masterâ€™s student. The following summer, I ended up fast-tracking into the PhD program. The overarching goal of my current research is to determine how the small translation repressor, 4E-BP1/2, regulates neurodevelopment and cell function to influence mental health and treatment. To reach this goal, we employ several different techniques including animal behaviour, immunohistochemistry and immunofluorescence, western blot/SDS page and PCR. In the future, we are planning to implement electrophysiology and in vivo fiber photometry.

Mike Parkhill

Hi, my name is Mike. I am starting my PhD during the fall semester of 2023, however, I have been at Carleton since 2017. During my undergrad, I investigated the effect of an eIF2A knockout genotype on behaviour and protein expression in mice. Since then, I have spent the last couple of years using two monogenetic mouse models of autism to evaluate the potential pharmacotherapeutic effects of cannabidiol (CBD).
